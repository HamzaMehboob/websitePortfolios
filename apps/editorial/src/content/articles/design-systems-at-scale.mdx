A design system is not a Figma library — it is the contract between brand, engineering, and product velocity.

## Why systems fail early

Teams often ship a component kit before they agree on **naming**, **spacing rhythm**, and **accessibility baselines**. The UI looks consistent in demos but fragments under real data density.

## Tokens before components

Start with semantic tokens: surface, border, text-muted, focus-ring. Map primitives once, then let components consume semantics. When brand refreshes, you update tokens — not forty button variants.

### Governance without bureaucracy

- **RFC-lite** for new patterns (one-page proposal)
- **Deprecation windows** for breaking changes
- **Visual regression** on critical flows only

## Measuring adoption

Track component usage in code, not slide decks. If teams fork buttons weekly, your API is wrong — not your evangelism.

## Closing thought

Scale is a people problem dressed as a tooling problem. Systems win when they reduce decisions, not when they catalog every pixel.
