Great editorial typography disappears — readers feel pace and tone without noticing CSS.

## Measure and rhythm

Body copy between **60–75 characters** per line aids comprehension. Vertical rhythm: line-height 1.6–1.75 for paragraphs; tighter for UI chrome.

## Hierarchy without shouting

Use weight and size before color alone. H2/H3 steps should be obvious when squinting. Link styles need underline or other non-color cues for color-blind readers.

### Drop caps and pull quotes

Use sparingly. A pull quote breaks scan patterns — place after the thesis paragraph, not before context exists.

## Dark mode considerations

Reduce contrast slightly vs pure white-on-black. Tint backgrounds (`#faf9f7` / `#171717`) to soften glare for long sessions.

Typography is the voice of the publication. Tune it like audio — levels, not effects.
